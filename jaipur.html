Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar, an ancient astronomical observatory in Jaipur, India, was built by Maharaja Jai Singh II in the early 18th century. Its collection of massive instruments, including sundials and celestial devices, were used for accurate timekeeping, tracking celestial bodies, and predicting eclipses. Today, it stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ajmer Fort

Amer Fort, a majestic fortress in Jaipur, Rajasthan, stands as a symbol of Rajput grandeur and architecture. Built in the late 16th century by Raja Man Singh I, it blends Hindu and Mughal architectural styles. With its intricate palaces, stunning courtyards, and panoramic views, it captivates visitors with its historical splendor.

City Palace

City Palace, a magnificent complex in Jaipur, Rajasthan, reflects the grandeur of Rajput and Mughal architecture. Built in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it comprises courtyards, gardens, and palaces like Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal. Today, it serves as a museum showcasing royal artifacts and history.

Naharagh Fort

Nahargarh Fort, an 18th-century marvel in Jaipur, Rajasthan, was constructed by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. Sitting atop the Aravalli hills, it served as a defensive bastion. With intricate palaces and stunning vistas of Jaipur, it remains a testament to Rajput architecture and offers a glimpse into the region's rich history.
